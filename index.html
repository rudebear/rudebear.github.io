Josh Humphreys - 3D, AR & VR Generalist

I have been working on various augmented and virtual reality projects since 2016 at Zubr.

Primarily my experience has been using Unity, C#, Autodesk Maya and Adobe Photoshop. I have some experience with using Unreal Engine 4, Agisoft Metashape, Pixologic Zbrush, Substance Suite and Quixel Mixer.

Here are some of the projects I have worked on at Zubr:

Eden Project Lost Giants Safari

A multi-user VR experience shown for a limited time at the Eden Project. I created particle systems in Unity using spritesheets supplied by an off-site contractor and in-office designer. I also imported and setup animal animations and created scripts to manage them, including animation events and pathing triggers.

AR Pirate Minigames

I scripted the game logic and core functionality of a series of pirate themed minigames for a private client, implementing 2D and 3D assets provided by artists"

Hidden Florence 3D: San Pier Maggiore AR

I created a low poly reconstruction of the San Pier Maggiore church based off of reference imagery and feedback from academic researchers.

Crack Magazine AR

By reducing and optimising existing photogrammetry, Aphex Twin came to life in an AR app using ARKit and ARCore. WeirdCore supplied frame-by-frame animations to transform the magazine into a music video.

I designed and created an out-of-this-world VR experience for the Eden Project, taking place on the surface of Venus. I used existing assets, as well as creating new ones to run on a Samsung GearVR using ARCore.

AR Treasure Hunt

Using Vuforia, a themed holiday newsletter spawned a world of animated characters and minigames. I created 3D models and particle effects, an achievement system to track the images scanned and the balloon minigame.

AR Cyber Security Game

I worked in a team of developers to create a cyber security themed AR game with data analytics to be shown at a convention. After adapting the CAD city to be used on iPads, I created effects to light up the 3D model or optionally the real tabletop as the game went on.

Human Body VR Installation

An on-rails VR journey through the human body was created using pre-existing CG models of anatomically correct cells. Using the Leap Motion, users could interact with objects within reach, allowing them to use their hands to hold the very cells in their body.

I recreated the now decommissioned Battersea Power Station control room, allowing users to peak back in time in an AR app. Check it out on my Artstation for a closer look.
